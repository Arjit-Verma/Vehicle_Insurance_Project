POLICY 2

Overview:

The Third-Party Liability Policy is a type of auto insurance that provides coverage for damages caused to third parties by your vehicle. It is designed to protect you financially in case your vehicle causes bodily injury or property damage to others in an accident for which you are deemed responsible.

Coverage Details:

  • Bodily Injury Coverage: This aspect of the policy covers medical expenses, rehabilitation costs, and other related expenses for injuries sustained by third parties due to an accident involving your vehicle. It provides financial protection for the injured party's physical well-being.
  • Property Damage Coverage: This coverage pays for the repair or replacement costs of third-party property that has been damaged by your vehicle in an accident. It includes compensation for damage to vehicles, buildings, fences, or other structures.
  • Legal Expenses: In the event of a lawsuit arising from an accident, this policy may cover legal fees, court costs, and other related expenses incurred in defending yourself against third-party claims. It helps protect you from the financial burden of legal proceedings.
  • Personal Injury Protection (PIP): Some Third-Party Liability Policies may include Personal Injury Protection (PIP) coverage, which provides medical expenses and lost wages coverage for you and your passengers, regardless of fault. This additional coverage enhances the financial protection provided by the policy.

Cost:

The premium for the Third-Party Liability Policy is typically priced at 12000rs/year. The cost may vary depending on factors such as the value of your vehicle, your driving history, the coverage limits, and the insurance provider's pricing structure.

Conclusion:

The Third-Party Liability Policy is essential for every vehicle owner as it provides financial protection against liabilities arising from accidents involving your vehicle. By opting for this policy, you ensure that you are compliant with legal requirements and have the necessary coverage to protect yourself and others in case of unforeseen events on the road.
